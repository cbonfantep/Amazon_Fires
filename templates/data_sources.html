Amazon fires: Cutting through the media noise


Data sources:

NASA

  • NASA provides insights into fires and thermal anomalies occurring daily around the world.
  • Satellite derived fire data and imagery available in Worldview are from the MODIS instrument on board the Terra and Aqua satellites and the VIIRS instrument on board the joint NASA/NOAA Suomi-NPP satellite.
  • Terra passes over the equator at approximately 10K30am (Day) and 10K30pm (Night) local time and Aqua and Suomi-NPP passes over the equator at approximately 1K30pm (Day) and 1K30am (Night) local time.
  • The fire information are available within Worldview approximately 3 hours after satellite overpass.

Satellite Data Collection and Data Rendering Details

  • MODIS=Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer
  • MODIS Data collected by the satellites utilize an algorithm that exploits the strong emission of mid-infrared radiation from fires and thermal anomalies.
  • For the MODIS instrument the point represents the center of a 1km pixel.
  • The fires and thermal anomalies are shown as red points on the NASA visualization imagery on the NASA FIRMS website.
  • We downloaded satellite fire detection data for the Brazilian Amazon into csv files grouped by year.
  • For our project visualization, we utilized code our team assembled to construct a heat map illustrating varying degrees of temperature, within a pre-selected geographical range determined by us.

    Satellite Data Collection and Data Rendering Details

    All nine satellites that have optical sensors operating in the mid-thermal range of 4um are used and that INPE can receive. At present (September / 2019), the images of the polar satellites, AVHRR / 3 of NOAA-18, NOAA-19 and METOP are processed operationally in the Imaging Division - DGI and the Satellite and Environmental Systems Division - DSA. -B, the NASA TERRA and AQUA MODIS and the NPP-Suomi and NOAA-20 VIIRS, and the geostationary satellite imagery GOES-16 and MSG-3.

    The following conditions greatly hinder or impair the detection of burns:
    • Fire fronts less than 30 m;
    • Fire only on the ground of a dense forest, without affecting the treetops;
    • Clouds covering the region;
    • Burn of short duration, occurring between the time of the available images;
    • Fire on a mountain slope, while the satellite only observed the other side;
    • Inaccuracy in the location of the firing focus, which in the best case is about 375 m but reaching 6 km.
